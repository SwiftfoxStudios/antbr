Bookshelf

what i've been reading

Weapons of Math Destruction

Cathy O'Neil

O'Neil, a mathematician, analyses how the use of big data and algorithms in a variety of fields, including insurance, advertising, education, and policing, can lead to decisions that harm the poor, reinforce racism, and amplify inequality.

Gödel, Escher, Bach: an Eternal Golden Braid

Douglas Hofstadter

Through short stories, illustrations, and analysis of the works of logician Kurt Gödel, artist M. C. Escher, and composer Johann Sebastian Bach, the book discusses how systems can acquire meaningful context despite being made of "meaningless" elements.

The 48 Laws Of Power

Robert Greene

Amoral, cunning, ruthless, and instructive, this piercing work distils three thousand years of the history of power into forty-eight well-explicated laws.

Ask A Historian

Greg Jenner

Jenner uses all the questions you never dared to ask about history as an excellent excuse to rummage around in some funny, fascinating, and downright peculiar corners of the past.
